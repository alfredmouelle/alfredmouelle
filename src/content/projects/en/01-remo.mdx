# Remo — note-taking built around your workflow

## The product

**Remo** is a note-taking app I design and build in public. The starting point is simple: most note apps are either too lightweight to get real work done, or too heavy and slow. Remo aims for the sweet spot — **simple by default, powerful when needed**.

The goal comes down to three steps: **capture** an idea in seconds, **structure** notes without friction, then **ship** decisions using AI workflows grounded in your own content.

![The Remo web app note editor](../_assets/remo-editor.webp)

## Key features

- **Instant capture** — Create a note in under three seconds, straight from the Raycast extension, without breaking your flow.
- **Context-aware assistant** — Summarize, reformulate, translate and extract todos through a multi-provider AI layer.
- **Vault & encryption** — Protect sensitive notes with lock controls and optional end-to-end encryption.
- **Real-time sync** — Notes sync instantly across every device via Convex.
- **Data portability** — Free import/export and sharable notes via secure links. Your data is yours.
- **History** — Snapshot and restore to roll back anytime.

## AI, grounded in your notes

Remo's assistant does more than generate text: it draws on the user's real content to answer questions, summarize, or extract concrete actions. The AI layer is abstracted behind a multi-provider interface (Anthropic, OpenAI, Gemini, Ollama), so switching models never means rewriting business logic.

## Architecture & tech stack

Remo is a **Turborepo + pnpm monorepo** bringing several surfaces together around a single backend:

- **Frontend**: TanStack Start (React 19), Shadcn UI and Tailwind CSS.
- **Backend & database**: Convex (serverless, real-time).
- **Authentication**: Clerk.
- **AI**: Vercel AI SDK, as a multi-provider abstraction.
- **Editor**: Tiptap.
- **Surfaces**: web app (with offline PWA support), an Electron desktop app (macOS / Windows) and a Raycast extension.
